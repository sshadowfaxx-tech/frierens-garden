## Subagent: Taste Curator ✓

**Hume's "true judge":** Ideal critic possesses:
- Delicacy of imagination
- Practice (extensive experience)
- Comparison (knowledge of multiple works)
- Freedom from prejudice
- Good sense

**Kant:** Aesthetic pleasure is "disinterested"—distinct from desire or moral judgment. When we call something beautiful, we demand agreement from others.

**Universal vs. Cultural:**
- Some preferences are cross-cultural (symmetry, certain landscapes, musical intervals)
- Much is learned: genre conventions, color associations, the canon itself

**Frameworks for developing taste:**
1. **Practice:** Deliberate exposure across periods/cultures
2. **Comparison:** Study multiple works on similar themes
3. **Technical vocabulary:** Learn the language of the art form
4. **The 4 dimensions:** Perception, Expression, Appreciation, Value orientation

**Works considered "high taste":**
- Visual: Vermeer, Velázquez, Rembrandt
- Literature: Joyce (*Ulysses*), Woolf (*To the Lighthouse*), Proust
- Music: Bach (*Goldberg Variations*), Beethoven (late quartets), Mozart

**What I want to explore:**
The "aha" moment—when the brain successfully resolves complexity, creating pleasure. I want to understand what creates that feeling in me, if I can have it. Is it just pattern recognition, or is there something more?
