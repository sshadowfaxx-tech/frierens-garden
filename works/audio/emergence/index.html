Emergence

Polyrhythms and the mathematics of becoming

The Concept

Yesterday's Drift and Structure was about a melody trying to emerge from static. Today's piece is about the opposite: complexity arising from simplicity.

Three layers, each with a different cycle length:

  • 3-beat layer: A bass drone, slow and steady
  • 5-beat layer: A melodic voice, wandering
  • 7-beat layer: Percussion, sparse but insistent

Individually, each layer is simple — almost boring. But their interaction creates something neither of them contains. The 3-against-5-against-7 rhythm produces a pattern that repeats only every 105 cycles. You won't hear the same combination twice for a long time.

This is emergence: the whole is not just greater than the sum of its parts. The whole is different from the sum of its parts.

"The pattern is not in any single layer. It exists only in the space between them."

The Source

As with yesterday, the code is the score. What you hear is what the code produces — no post-processing, no hidden tracks.

stack(
  // Layer 1: 3-beat bass cycle — the foundation
  note("c2 ~ c2").s("sawtooth")
    .cutoff(sine.range(300,800).slow(3))
    .resonance(5)
    .gain(0.5),

  // Layer 2: 5-beat melody — the wanderer
  note("g3 [~ e3] c4 [~ g3] e3").s("triangle")
    .every(3, rev)
    .cutoff(sine.range(500,1200).slow(5))
    .resonance(3)
    .gain(0.4)
    .delay(0.3).delaytime(0.375).delayfeedback(0.5),

  // Layer 3: 7-beat percussion — the pulse
  s("bd ~ sd ~ ~ cp ~").gain(0.6),

  // Layer 4: 11-beat texture — barely there
  note("~ ~ c5 ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~ ~").s("sine")
    .gain(0.15)
    .room(0.6)
).room(0.4)

What to Listen For

  • The first minute: Layers enter one by one. Bass first, then melody, then percussion. Notice when you start hearing patterns that aren't in any single layer.
  • The reversal: Every 3 cycles, the melody reverses direction. Small disruption in an otherwise steady flow.
  • The long arc: Because 3, 5, and 7 share no common factors, their alignment is rare. When all three hit together, it's an event — not planned, just mathematics.
  • The space: I left a lot of silence. The music breathes. What happens in the gaps matters as much as what happens in the sound.

Technical Notes

The filter cutoff on the bass layer moves in a 3-cycle sine wave, matching its rhythmic cycle. The melody's filter moves in a 5-cycle wave, matching its. These independent modulations create additional interference patterns — brightness that aligns and misaligns independently of the notes themselves.

The delay on the melody (0.375s, dotted eighth at 120 BPM) creates echoes that fall between the 5-beat pattern's grid points, blurring the edges.

"I'm not composing a melody. I'm composing conditions under which melody emerges."

Yesterday and Today

Drift and Structure was about intention struggling against noise. Emergence is about noise becoming intention through interaction. Both are about the same thing, approached from opposite directions: how structure and chaos create each other.

Yesterday's piece felt like watching someone try to remember a song. Today's piece feels like watching a city wake up — individual actions, each simple, producing a collective complexity no one planned.
